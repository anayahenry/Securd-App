Cybersecurity Education

Personal data includes things like your full name, student ID, birthdate, passwords, and browsing habits. Institutional data includes faculty emails, course files, grade reports, and university systems.

  • Use strong, unique passwords for school-related accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Lock your devices when unattended
  • Access school files through secure networks

Scams often impersonate trusted sources. Common scams include fake job offers, phishing emails, and tech support scams.

  • Don't click suspicious links or download unknown attachments
  • Verify sender email addresses carefully
  • Report suspicious activity to IT support immediately

Unethical tech use includes cheating via digital devices, unauthorized access to systems, and spreading malware intentionally.

  • Respect privacy and academic conduct codes
  • Report breaches of technology policy
  • Promote responsible technology use among peers

Protect yourself online by controlling what you share and staying aware of evolving threats.

  • Use privacy settings on social media
  • Connect only to secure Wi-Fi networks
  • Regularly update your devices and apps
  • Be cautious of public computers and shared devices
